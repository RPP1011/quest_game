It arrived with the heavy, measured cadence of a giant's breath, a throb of red so intense it seemed to stain the very water from the inside out. The color was the hue of fresh arterial blood or a sun dying in a sea of wine, and it surged upward in timed intervals, turning the violet swells into translucent, glowing lanterns.

"Look!" Tristan shouted, his voice cracking as he pointed toward the churning wake. The core of the building is turning red!"

The light didn't just illuminate the water; it seemed to claim it. Every time the pulse hit, the pressure in the air spiked, a sudden surge of warmth that felt like the heat of a stoked furnace pressing against their skin. The red light was thick, visceral, and it rose in great, billowing clouds that swirled around the hull like the hem of a heavy velvet robe being dragged through the sand.

Angharad watched the light with a stillness that defied the shuddering deck. The crimson radiance hit the underside of the ship and washed upward, casting long, dancing shadows of the rigging across her face. It was a predatory light, a warning written in the language of heat and salt. She felt the Fisher's influence in the way the light seemed to linger in her vision, a ghost-image that pulsed behind her eyelids even when she blinked.

"It's the Maw," she murmured, the word feeling heavy and ancient on her tongue. 

The rhythmic thudding of the light began to synchronize with the grinding groan of the deep. Each pulse of red was accompanied by a surge of motion, a heave of the sea that pushed the *Bluebell* higher into the curve of the world before the gravity of the sinkhole pulled them back down. The ship was caught in a tug-of-war between the descent and the rising tide of the red light, a frantic oscillation that made the masts sway like reeds in a gale.

Tristan scrambled toward the railing, his eyes fixed on the glow. The water was no longer just a liquid; it was a living, breathing thing, a vast lung of red fire beneath the violet skin of the sea. The light felt like a heavy hand pressing against the bottom of the boat, pushing upward with a strength that promised to eventually shatter the wood and the bone alike.
