"It's a slide," Tristan grunted, his hands white-knuckled on the brass. Like the threads in a woven fabric, only we're the fibers, and the loom has just been turned."

The crimson light from below flared, brighter now, pulsing in time with the heavy, rhythmic thrumming in their architecture. As the ship tilted deeper into the slope, the light grew more intense, turning the violet water into a translucent, glowing garnet. It was no longer a distant glow; it was a presence, a vast and hungry thing rising to meet them.

The *Bluebell* groaned, a long, wood-splitting sound that echoed the deep hum of the ocean. The tilt was relentless, a steady, inevitable descent into the heart of the pulse. For a moment, Angharad felt the terrifying sensation of emptiness, as if the ship were a hollow vessel, suspended between the air they breathed and the light that was coming to claim them.

Then, the slope steepened. 

The ship didn't just lean; it began to plunge, the bow dipping into the glowing red abyss, and for the first time, Angharad did not reach for a handhold. She reached for the foresight, waiting to see if the census had missed the most important thing: the moment the mountain finally tumbled.
